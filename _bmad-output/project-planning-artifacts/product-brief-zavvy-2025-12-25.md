# Product Brief: zavvy

<!-- Content will be appended sequentially through collaborative workflow steps -->

## Executive Summary

Zavvy is an intelligent scheduling assistant that lives in WhatsApp, designed for freelancers and small businesses in Brazil. Unlike traditional scheduling tools that require behavior change, Zavvy integrates into the professional's existing workflow—where WhatsApp is already the primary communication channel.

The core insight: professionals don't need another tool to manage. They need someone to handle the operational burden while they stay in control. Zavvy delivers exactly that—automated scheduling with a personal assistant experience, not cold software.

**Key differentiator:** Professionals use Zavvy's centralized WhatsApp Business infrastructure—no need to set up their own WhatsApp Business account. Zero friction onboarding.

**Key Value Proposition:** "Tools organize calendars. Zavvy takes care of yours."

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Freelancers and small business owners who sell their time face an invisible cost: the burden of manual scheduling. They lose hours responding to repetitive WhatsApp messages, lose focus as scheduling interrupts their core work, and lose opportunities due to scheduling conflicts, slow responses, or disorganization.

WhatsApp became the primary contact channel in Brazil, but it was never designed to manage calendars. The result is a manual, exhausting, and unscalable process.

### Problem Impact

**Practical consequences:**
- Lost clients due to slow response times
- Overbooking and scheduling conflicts
- Appointments outside preferred hours
- Poorly managed cancellations
- Constant feeling of chaos

**Emotional consequence (the most important):**
- Mental exhaustion and stress
- Feeling of always "putting out fires"
- Difficulty scaling without burnout
- The professional adapts to tools instead of tools adapting to them

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Solution | Why It Fails |
|----------|--------------|
| **WhatsApp + Memory** | No calendar view, no conflict prevention, doesn't scale |
| **Google Calendar** | Doesn't communicate with clients, requires constant discipline |
| **Calendly/Similar** | Lives outside WhatsApp, feels cold and corporate, doesn't negotiate or understand context, solves the slot but not the experience |

**The fundamental gap:** Existing tools require the professional to do the work. They organize information but don't reduce operational burden.

> "In the end, I'm the one who has to take care of everything."

### Proposed Solution

Zavvy is a WhatsApp-native intelligent scheduling assistant that:

- **Lives where the professional already is** — no new apps to learn
- **Converses naturally** — understands context, exceptions, and preferences
- **Automates the operation** — blocks calendars, sends confirmations, manages notifications
- **Keeps the professional in control** — they define the rules, Zavvy executes
- **Zero WhatsApp setup required** — professionals use Zavvy's centralized WhatsApp Business infrastructure (no need to configure their own WABA)

The professional stays in control without the manual work. The end client perceives a more organized, professional service—even though the professional is operating solo.

**Core positioning:** "Zavvy handles the operation. You define the rules."

### Key Differentiators

1. **WhatsApp-First (Cultural Fit)**
   - WhatsApp in Brazil isn't "a channel"—it's THE channel
   - No behavior change required
   - Fits the existing habit perfectly

2. **Zero Setup WhatsApp Infrastructure**
   - Professionals use Zavvy's centralized WABA—no need to configure their own WhatsApp Business
   - Dramatically simpler onboarding vs. competitors that require WABA setup
   - Zavvy controls quality, compliance, and message templates

3. **Assistant, Not Tool**
   - Feels like someone helping, not software to configure
   - Continuous support sensation vs. cold automation
   - "Tools organize calendars. Zavvy takes care of yours."

4. **Control Without Effort**
   - Professional defines rules and preferences
   - Zavvy handles execution and exceptions
   - Reduces fear of "losing control to AI"

5. **Perceived Professionalism**
   - Fast responses, clear confirmations, organized calendar
   - End clients see a professional who "has their act together"
   - Psychological benefit: appear larger and more organized than reality

## Target Users

### Primary Users

#### Core Persona: Solo Professional

**Profile:**
- Works alone or with minimal support
- Age range: 25-50 years old
- Provides specialized services where time equals money
- Income depends directly on their schedule
- Doesn't see themselves as a "company" but is no longer informal

**Examples:**
- Psychologists, therapists, coaches
- Independent lawyers, consultants
- Hairdressers, manicurists, massage therapists
- Personal trainers, nutritionists
- Designers, photographers

**Daily Reality:**
- Appointments concentrated in blocks throughout the day
- Irregular breaks between clients
- Constant WhatsApp messages: "Do you have time this week?", "Can we reschedule?", "Please confirm"
- Updates calendar between appointments or late at night
- Constantly alternating between: serving clients, organizing schedule, responding to messages

**Core Pain:**
- Feeling always behind
- Fear of double-booking or missing appointments
- Mental exhaustion from negotiating schedules
- No time to think about growth

**What They Value:**
- Fewer messages to respond to
- Fewer repetitive decisions
- Control without micromanagement
- Sense of organization and professionalism

> **Golden Phrase:** "Zavvy is for professionals who are already too busy to keep organizing their schedule manually—but still too small to hire a secretary."

#### Persona Variations

| Variation | Description | Priority |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Solo Professional** | One calendar, one WhatsApp, quick decisions, high pain, low setup tolerance | MVP Core |
| **Small Structure** | Small clinic/studio with 2-5 professionals, more volume, more rules, may have informal receptionist | Post-MVP |
| **Hybrid Professional** | Part in-person, part remote, frequent schedule changes, many exceptions | Future (requires product maturity) |

#### Anti-Persona (Who Zavvy is NOT for)

- Large companies with enterprise software
- Large clinics with ERP systems
- Users who dislike WhatsApp
- Those without significant appointment volume
- Those who want extreme manual control

### Secondary Users

| User | Relationship | Benefit |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **End Client** | Books appointments via link | Faster responses, less confusion, perceived professionalism |
| **Receptionist/Assistant** | Future: handles only exceptions | Less repetitive work, Zavvy as "first line of defense" |
| **Business Owner** | If professional works for someone | Less chaos, lower operational costs |

### User Journey

#### 1. Discovery — "I can't take this anymore"

**Channels (realistic order):**
1. **Instagram** — Simple ads ("schedule via WhatsApp"), stories from other professionals, educational content
2. **Referral** — From another professional in the same segment, WhatsApp groups, courses
3. **Google** — "how to organize WhatsApp schedule", "appointment system for [profession]"

**Trigger Moment:**
- Lost a client due to slow response
- Double-booked two clients
- Worked outside preferred hours without realizing
- Received messages at 2 AM
- Spent the whole day just responding to WhatsApp

> **Key thought:** "I'm making money, but this schedule is killing me."

#### 2. Onboarding — "Is this complicated?"

**Ideal First Experience:**
- Simple landing page
- Understands in 30 seconds: "It's like a secretary on WhatsApp"
- Clicks "Start Free"

**Setup Flow (Web-First):**
1. Register via landing page (email, password, name, profession)
2. Choose plan (trial or paid) and confirm billing
3. Connect Google Calendar or Outlook (OAuth)
4. Create services with name, duration, buffer time (structured forms)
5. Configure availability (visual interface: days, hours, breaks)
6. Activate WhatsApp connection (last step)
7. Execute first action via WhatsApp (mandatory)

**Time to Value:** Less than 10 minutes

> **Key:** Web handles complexity (setup, configuration). WhatsApp handles daily operation. No WhatsApp Business setup required—professionals use Zavvy's infrastructure.

#### 3. "Aha!" Moment — "This actually works"

**The True Value Moment:**
Not connecting the calendar. It's when they send their first message to Zavvy:

> "Zavvy, how's my schedule tomorrow?"

And Zavvy responds instantly with their full agenda. They think: **"I don't need to open the system to manage my schedule anymore."**

**Other "Aha" Moments:**
- Notices fewer repetitive messages
- No scheduling conflicts that day
- Next day's schedule is already organized
- Client arrived knowing the exact time

> **Feeling:** "Someone is taking care of this for me."

#### 4. Daily Use — "It just flows now"

**How Zavvy Fits the Routine:**
- The professional doesn't think about Zavvy
- They just: attend clients, receive notifications, adjust exceptions when needed

**What Changes:**
| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Responding to WhatsApp constantly | Notifications only |
| Manually checking calendar | Already organized |
| Constant stress | Mental peace |

> **Invisible but profound change.**

#### 5. Long-term — "Can't live without it anymore"

**Why They Stay:**
- Becomes part of the routine
- Schedule works effortlessly
- Stopping = returning to chaos

> **Zavvy becomes infrastructure, not a tool.**

**What Drives Recommendations:**
- Another professional complains about scheduling
- Natural response: "I use a WhatsApp secretary, changed everything."

> **Organic, emotional referrals—not technical.**

#### Journey Summary

**The user arrives exhausted, tests skeptically, is surprised quickly, and stays because life becomes simpler.**

## MVP Scope

### MVP Definition

Zavvy MVP is a WhatsApp-first intelligent scheduling SaaS with service-based booking, recurring subscription, professional web app, and admin panel for operations, compliance, and scale.

**Architecture:**
- **WhatsApp** = Where value happens (via Zavvy's centralized WABA)
- **Web App** = Where users trust and see the business
- **Subscription** = How the product sustains itself
- **Admin** = How Zavvy scales

**WhatsApp Model (MVP):**
- Zavvy operates a **centralized WhatsApp Business API (WABA)**
- Professionals connect to Zavvy's infrastructure—no need to set up their own WhatsApp Business
- Simpler onboarding, Zavvy controls quality and compliance
- **Post-MVP:** Option for professionals to connect their own WABA (Pro plan)

### Core Features

#### Core 1: Intelligent WhatsApp Assistant

**The Value Engine (via Zavvy's centralized WABA):**
- Understand intent: schedule / reschedule / cancel
- Converse in natural language
- Offer available time slots
- Confirm appointments
- Block calendar automatically
- Notify the professional

> **Note:** Professionals interact with Zavvy's WhatsApp infrastructure. No need to configure their own WhatsApp Business account.

#### Core 2: Service-Based Scheduling

**Service Configuration:**
- Service name
- Duration (e.g., 1h, 3h, 5h)
- Optional buffer time
- Price (optional but structural)

**AI Capabilities:**
- Identify requested service
- Calculate possible time windows
- Prevent conflicts

> **Differentiator:** This separates Zavvy from 90% of competitors.

#### Core 3: Calendar Integrations

**MVP Integrations:**
- Google Calendar
- Outlook Calendar

**Minimum Functionality:**
- Read availability
- Create events
- Prevent conflicts

#### Core 4: Professional Web App

**Why It's Essential:**
- Builds trust
- Builds audience
- Increases retention
- Justifies subscription

**Features:**

| Section | Functionality |
|---------|---------------|
| **Calendar** | View schedule, appointment status |
| **Clients (Light CRM)** | Client list, WhatsApp, service history |
| **Services** | Create/edit services, duration, rules |
| **Settings** | Working hours, availability rules, basic automations |

#### Core 5: SaaS Subscription

**Not optional—launches from day one.**

**Starter Plan:**
- 1 professional account
- Access to Zavvy's WhatsApp infrastructure (centralized WABA)
- Google + Outlook integration
- Unlimited services
- Unlimited clients
- Web app included
- Automatic scheduling

**Price:** R$39-59/month

> **Note:** Professionals don't need their own WhatsApp Business—they use Zavvy's centralized infrastructure. Own WABA connection available in future Pro plan.

#### Core 6: Proper Paywall

**Payment Flow:**
1. User signs up
2. Uses Zavvy
3. Sees real value
4. Activates subscription
5. Continues without friction

**If Not Paying:**
- New automatic appointments disabled
- Automatic WhatsApp responses disabled

**Keeps Access To:**
- Web app (read-only)
- Client data
- History
- Calendar view

> **Goal:** Avoid anger, increase conversion.

#### Core 7: Zavvy Admin Panel

**Required for WhatsApp SaaS operations.**

| Section | Functionality |
|---------|---------------|
| **Client Management** | Professional list, status (active/trial/cancelled), current plan |
| **WhatsApp Templates** | Create/manage approved templates, Meta compliance |
| **Billing** | Payment status, activate/deactivate, grant trial/discount, logs |
| **Monitoring** | Failed conversations, scheduling errors |

> **This is infrastructure, not a feature.**

### Out of Scope for MVP

**Explicitly NOT included:**
- Payments within WhatsApp
- Marketing campaigns
- Advanced CRM
- Multi-professional support
- Multi-location support
- Advanced reports/BI
- Long-term AI memory
- Deep message customization
- **Professional's own WhatsApp Business connection** (MVP uses Zavvy's centralized WABA)

> **Rationale:** These don't validate the core value proposition.

### MVP Success Criteria

#### User Success Indicators

- Pays without complaining
